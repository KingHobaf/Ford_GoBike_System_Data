Investigation Overview¶

In this presentation, my primary focus is primarily on the factors that predicts how long a rider will ride a bike (duration) .

Dataset Overview¶

Ford GoBike System Data exploration describes the exploration of data of riders obtained in a bike-sharing system covering the greater San Francisco Bay area. In this dataset, I will draw useful insight on predictors of how long a rider can ride.

Visualization 4: Which day of the week do subscribers and customers prefer to ride bikes?¶

  • For subscribers, they prefer to ride bike on Thursday, so also are the customers too.
  • The order for the most preferred week days for subscriber follows the order: Thur > Tue > Wed > Fri > Mon > Sat~Sun. They prefer to ride on Thursdays but not frequently on Saturdays or Sundays.
  • for the customers, the order for the most preferred week days follows : Thur > Fri > Tue > Mon > Sun > Wed ~ Sat. They prefer to ride on Thursdays but not frequently on Wednesday and Saturdays.

# Write a function to avoid repetition of code
def multi_catnum(num, cat1, cat2, kind, height, col_wrap):
    
    if kind == "bar" and height== None and col_wrap ==None:
        plt.figure(figsize=(13,9))
        ax = sb.barplot(data = bike_clean, x = cat1, y = num, hue = cat2, palette='rocket_r')
        plt.setp(ax.axes, xlabel= cat1, ylabel= num);
        plt.title(f"Effect of Biker's {cat1} and {cat2} on {num}");
     
        
    elif kind == "barf" and height == height and col_wrap==col_wrap:
        g=sb.FacetGrid(data= bike_clean, col = cat1, margin_titles=True, col_wrap=col_wrap, height =height)
        g.map(sb.barplot, num, cat2, color='purple')
        g.fig.subplots_adjust(top=0.89);
        g.fig.suptitle(f"Effect of Biker's {cat1} and {cat2} on {num}");
        plt.show()
